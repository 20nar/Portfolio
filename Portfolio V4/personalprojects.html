Personal Project

Remy Skate Video

Since I was 13 years old I started skateboarding and shooting videos for skateboarders. Back in the day I only used my phone's camera to shoot all the videos. I always wanted to use a professional camera and gear to produce videos, but never had the opportunity to do so.

I wanted to make a skateboard documentary, but this would have taken way too long. That is why I decided to make a promo video for a skateboarder.

In this video I filmed a friend of mine that I knew from Curaçao. Remy, 18 years old now, is such an amazing skateboarder. This skater has no fear when it comes to big drops and crazy tricks.

Unfortunately I could only film Remy once in Spoorpark. I did film him another time but that was at a different location. I wanted to keep it in Spoorpark

In the first selfie week I could not film Remy, because he was too busy with his school and he lives in Belgium. I made this video in the second selfie week and created a brandguide with skateboards for him as well.


Shot and edited with:

DJI Osmo 2 & Samsung A7

In order for me to keep up with the skateboarder I used a stabilizer to always keep the camera steady.Using the wide angle lens of the Samsung a7 I was able to get the skateboarder fully in the shot every time.

Canon EOS 650D

In this video clip I also wanted to get some shots of the city and some close up shots of the skateboarders. I used the standard Canon EF-S 18-55mm F/3.5-5.6 II occasion lens.

Adobe Premiere 2020

This was my fist time actually using Adobe Premiere. My previous work I usualy did in Filmora Pro. Premiere is so much easier and there so many different things you can use. .

Conclusion

Working with skateboarders is quite a challenge. I learned that filming with them never really goes as planned. Remy never had time and I realized that I should have filmed another skateboarder so I could make a better video. Because I did not get to film an interview with him. By conducting an interview with Remy I could have made the video more about him. Now it is just a skateboard video that shows his tricks. But nobody know who Remy really is. At leat you cannot get that from the video.

I did learn a lot with Adobe Premiere Pro, it is such a powerful program and I did not even get to use all the features that is has. I would love to film a lot more and espescially skateboarders.

Next to that I also learned a lot with Adobe illustrator. The patterns in the designs were difficult but fun to make.
